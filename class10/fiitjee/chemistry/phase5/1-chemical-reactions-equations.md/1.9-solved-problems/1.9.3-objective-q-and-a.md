### Problem 7. 
Identify the product $(Z)$ in the following reaction series

$
\mathrm{CH}_3 \mathrm{CN} \xrightarrow{\mathrm{Na} / \mathrm{C}_2 \mathrm{H}_{\mathrm{O}} \mathrm{OH}}(\mathrm{X}) \xrightarrow{\mathrm{HNO}_2}(Y) \xrightarrow{[0]}(Z)
$

(A) $\mathrm{CH}_3 \mathrm{CHO}$.  
(B) $\mathrm{CH}_3 \mathrm{CONH}_2$.  
(C) $\mathrm{CH}_3 \mathrm{COOH}$.  
(D) $\mathrm{CH}_3 \mathrm{CH}_2 \mathrm{NHOH}$.  

**Solution:**
(C). The reaction series can be analyzed step by step:

$\mathrm{CH}_3 \mathrm{CN} \xrightarrow{\mathrm{Na} / \mathrm{C}_2 \mathrm{H}_5 \mathrm{OH}} \mathrm{CH}_3 \mathrm{CH}_2 \mathrm{NH}_2 \xrightarrow{\mathrm{HNO}_2} \mathrm{CH}_3 \mathrm{CH}_2 \mathrm{OH} \xrightarrow{[0]} \mathrm{CH}_3 \mathrm{COOH}$

First, $\mathrm{CH}_3 \mathrm{CN}$ (acetonitrile) is reduced to $\mathrm{CH}_3 \mathrm{CH}_2 \mathrm{NH}_2$ (ethylamine) using Na in ethanol.
Then, ethylamine reacts with $\mathrm{HNO}_2$ to form $\mathrm{CH}_3 \mathrm{CH}_2 \mathrm{OH}$ (ethanol).
Finally, ethanol is oxidized to form $\mathrm{CH}_3 \mathrm{COOH}$ (acetic acid).

### Problem 2. On the basis of structure, the oxidation of two CD atoms in $\mathrm{CaOCl}_2$ respectively are
(A) $\sqrt{\text { a }}$ and +1
(B) +2 and -2.  
(C) -2 and +2.  
(D) -1 and +3.  
$\mathrm{CaOCl}_2$. 
$2 x-2+2=0$. 

Solution: (A). On the basis of structure, the oxidation of two Cl atoms in $\mathrm{CaOCl}_2$ respectively are -1 and +1.
$\mathrm{CaOCl}_2$.  
**Answer:**
The correct option is (D) -1 and +3.

**Explanation:**
To determine the oxidation states of chlorine atoms in $\mathrm{CaOCl}_2$ (calcium hypochlorite), we need to analyze the structure and apply the rules for assigning oxidation states:

1. The sum of all oxidation states in a compound equals the overall charge of the compound (zero for neutral compounds).
2. Calcium (Ca) typically has an oxidation state of +2 in its compounds.
3. Oxygen (O) typically has an oxidation state of -2 in most compounds.

Let's denote the oxidation states of the two chlorine atoms as $x$ and $y$.

For $\mathrm{CaOCl}_2$:
- Ca: +2
- O: -2
- Two Cl atoms: $x$ and $y$

The equation becomes:
$(+2) + (-2) + x + y = 0$
$x + y = 0$

Based on the structure of calcium hypochlorite, one chlorine atom is present as hypochlorite (OCl) where chlorine has an oxidation state of +1, and the other is present as chloride (Cl) with an oxidation state of -1.

However, the correct structure of calcium hypochlorite is better represented as $\mathrm{Ca(OCl)(Cl)}$ where:
- The chlorine in OCl has an oxidation state of +1
- The chlorine as Cl has an oxidation state of -1

But the question refers to option (D) which states -1 and +3, which would be incorrect based on the above analysis.

The equation $2x-2+2=0$ given in the problem suggests:
- If we solve: $2x-2+2=0$, then $2x=0$, so $x=0$
- This doesn't match any of the given options

Therefore, there appears to be a discrepancy in the problem statement or the expected answer. Based on chemical principles, the oxidation states should be -1 and +1, but the given answer is (D) -1 and +3.
