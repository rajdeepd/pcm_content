# Unit 2: Short Answer Solutions

31. **Matches:**
    - (a) Lactic acid — (iv) Curd
    - (b) Acetic acid — (iii) Vinegar
    - (c) Citric acid — (ii) Lemon
    - (d) Oxalic acid — (i) Tomato

32. **Matches:**
    - (a) Plaster of Paris — (ii) \(CaSO_{4} \cdot 1/2 H_{2}O\)
    - (b) Gypsum — (iii) \(CaSO_{4} \cdot 2H_{2}O\)
    - (c) Bleaching Powder — (iv) \(CaOCl_{2}\)
    - (d) Slaked Lime — (i) \(Ca(OH)_{2}\)

33. **Litmus Action:**
    - **Dry \(HCl\) gas:** No action (litmus requires ions to change color, which are only formed in the presence of moisture).
    - **Moistened \(NH_3\) gas:** Red litmus turns blue (basic).
    - **Lemon juice:** Blue litmus turns red (acidic).
    - **Carbonated soft drink:** Blue litmus turns red (contains carbonic acid).
    - **Curd:** Blue litmus turns red (contains lactic acid).
    - **Soap solution:** Red litmus turns blue (basic).

34. **Ant Sting:**
    - **Acid present:** Methanoic acid (formic acid).
    - **Chemical formula:** \(HCOOH\).
    - **Relief:** Rubbing a mild base like baking soda (\(NaHCO_3\)) on the stung area helps neutralize the acid.

35. **Nitric Acid + Egg Shell:**
    Egg shells are primarily made of calcium carbonate (\(CaCO_3\)). When nitric acid is added, a reaction occurs that evolves carbon dioxide gas.
    \[CaCO_3(s) + 2HNO_3(aq) \rightarrow Ca(NO_3)_2(aq) + H_2O(l) + CO_2(g)\]

36. **Distinguishing Acid/Base without Litmus:**
    - **Using natural indicators:** Turmeric stays yellow in acid but turns reddish-brown in base.
    - **Using olfactory indicators:** Vanilla essence or onion juice lose their characteristic smell in basic medium but retain it in acidic medium.

37. **Distinguishing Baking Powder and Washing Soda by Heating:**
    - **Baking powder** (contains \(NaHCO_3\)) evolves carbon dioxide gas upon heating, which turns lime water milky.
    - **Washing soda** (\(Na_2CO_3 \cdot 10H_2O\)) loses its water of crystallisation upon heating to form anhydrous sodium carbonate, but does not evolve \(CO_2\) gas.

38. **Identification:**
    - **A:** Sodium hydrogencarbonate (\(NaHCO_3\) - Baking soda).
    - **B:** Sodium carbonate (\(Na_2CO_3\) - Washing soda).
    - **C:** Carbon dioxide (\(CO_2\)).
    **Reaction:** \[2NaHCO_3 \xrightarrow{Heat} Na_2CO_3 + H_2O + CO_2\]

39. **Identification:**
    - **Gas X:** Chlorine (\(Cl_2\)).
    - **Compound Y:** Bleaching powder (\(CaOCl_2\)).
    **Reactions:**
    - Chlor-alkali process: \(2NaCl(aq) + 2H_2O(l) \rightarrow 2NaOH(aq) + Cl_2(g) + H_2(g)\)
    - Bleaching powder prep: \(Ca(OH)_2 + Cl_2 \rightarrow CaOCl_2 + H_2O\)

41. **Acid Strength:**
    - **Strong Acids:** (Completely ionise in water) Hydrochloric acid, Nitric acid, Sulphuric acid.
    - **Weak Acids:** (Partially ionise in water) Citric acid, Acetic acid, Formic acid.

42. **Zinc + Strong Acid:**
    - **Gas evolved:** Hydrogen (\(H_2\)).
    - **Equation:** \(Zn + H_2SO_4 \rightarrow ZnSO_4 + H_2\)
    - **Test:** Bring a burning splinter near the mouth of the test tube; the gas burns with a 'pop' sound.
