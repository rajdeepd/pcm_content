# 1.2 Balancing Chemical Equations

## Skeletal vs Balanced Equations
- Skeletal equation: atoms not equal on both sides (e.g., $ \text{C}_2\text{H}_6 + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow \text{CO}_2 + \text{H}_2\text{O} $).
- Balanced equation: atoms of each element equal on both sides, satisfying the law of conservation of mass.

## Hit-and-Trial Method (Examples)

### Ethane Combustion
- Start: $ \text{C}_2\text{H}_6 + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow \text{CO}_2 + \text{H}_2\text{O} $
- Balance H by multiplying $ \text{H}_2\text{O} $ by 3.  
- Balance C by multiplying $ \text{CO}_2 $ by 2.  
- Balance O by multiplying $ \text{O}_2 $ by 7/2, then scale all coefficients by 2 to keep whole numbers.  
- Balanced: $ 2\text{C}_2\text{H}_6 + 7\text{O}_2 \rightarrow 4\text{CO}_2 + 6\text{H}_2\text{O} $

### Fe + Steam
- Start: $ \text{Fe} + \text{H}_2\text{O} \rightarrow \text{Fe}_3\text{O}_4 + \text{H}_2 $
- Balance O by placing 4 before $ \text{H}_2\text{O} $.  
- Balance Fe by placing 3 before Fe.  
- Balance H by placing 4 before $ \text{H}_2 $.  
- Balanced: $ 3\text{Fe} + 4\text{H}_2\text{O} \rightarrow \text{Fe}_3\text{O}_4 + 4\text{H}_2 $

## Making Equations More Informative
- Add physical states: (s), (l), (g), (aq).
- Indicate precipitate (↓) or evolved gas (↑) when helpful.
- Note reaction conditions above/below the arrow (e.g., heat as $[\text{Heat}]$ or $ \Delta $, light, electricity, pressure, catalyst).
- Show heat absorbed/evolved on the product side for endothermic/exothermic changes, respectively.

### Examples of Notation
- Physical states: $ \text{Mg}(s) + \text{H}_2\text{SO}_4(aq) \rightarrow \text{MgSO}_4(aq) + \text{H}_2(g) \uparrow $
- Reaction conditions: $ \text{CO}(g) + 2\text{H}_2(g) \xrightarrow[340\ \text{atm}]{\text{Catalyst}} \text{CH}_3\text{OH}(l) $
- Heat change: $ \text{C}(s) + \text{O}_2(g) \rightarrow \text{CO}_2(g) + \text{Heat} $
