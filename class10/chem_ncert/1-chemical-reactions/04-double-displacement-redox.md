# 1.4 Double Displacement & Redox

## Double Displacement (Precipitation)
- Ions exchange partners; often a precipitate forms.  
- Examples:  
  - $ \text{Na}_2\text{SO}_4 + \text{BaCl}_2 \rightarrow \text{BaSO}_4 \downarrow + 2\text{NaCl} $  
  - $ \text{AgNO}_3 + \text{NaBr} \rightarrow \text{AgBr} \downarrow + \text{NaNO}_3 $

## Oxidation and Reduction
- **Oxidation:** addition of oxygen or removal of hydrogen.  
- **Reduction:** removal of oxygen or addition of hydrogen.
- Examples:  
  - Oxidation: $ 2\text{Cu} + \text{O}_2 \xrightarrow{\text{Heat}} 2\text{CuO} $  
  - Reduction: $ \text{CuO} + \text{H}_2 \xrightarrow{\text{Heat}} \text{Cu} + \text{H}_2\text{O} $

## Redox Reactions
- Oxidation and reduction occur simultaneously.  
- Examples:  
  - $ \text{ZnO} + \text{C} \rightarrow \text{Zn} + \text{CO} $ (ZnO reduced; C oxidised)  
  - $ \text{MnO}_2 + 4\text{HCl} \rightarrow \text{MnCl}_2 + \text{Cl}_2 + 2\text{H}_2\text{O} $ (HCl oxidised; MnO₂ reduced)

## Oxidising and Reducing Agents
- **Oxidising agent:** causes oxidation of another and is itself reduced (e.g., $ \text{Cl}_2 $, $ \text{MnO}_2 $).  
- **Reducing agent:** causes reduction of another and is itself oxidised (e.g., C, H₂).
