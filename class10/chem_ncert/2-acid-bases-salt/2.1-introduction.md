## Indicators

### What are Indicators?

Indicators are substances that show different colours in acidic and basic solutions. They help us identify whether a substance is an acid or a base.

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| **Natural Indicators** | Litmus, Turmeric, Red cabbage leaves, Coloured petals of flowers (Hydrangea, Petunia, Geranium) |
| **Synthetic Indicators** | Methyl orange, Phenolphthalein |
| **Olfactory Indicators** | Onion, Vanilla, Clove oil |

> **Do You Know?**
> 
> Litmus solution is a purple dye, which is extracted from lichen, a plant belonging to the division Thallophyta, and is commonly used as an indicator. When the litmus solution is neither acidic nor basic, its colour is purple.

## Summary of Indicator Colour Changes

| Indicator | In Acid | In Base |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Blue Litmus** | Turns Red | No change |
| **Red Litmus** | No change | Turns Blue |
| **Phenolphthalein** | Colourless | Pink |
| **Methyl Orange** | Red/Pink | Yellow |
| **Turmeric** | Yellow | Reddish-brown |

## Question

1. You have been provided with three test tubes. One of them contains distilled water and the other two contain an acidic solution and a basic solution, respectively. If you are given only red litmus paper, how will you identify the contents of each test tube?

### Answer
[cite_start]Based on the principles found in the text[cite: 509], here is the step-by-step approach to identify the contents of the three test tubes using only red litmus paper:

**1. Identify the Base**
* Dip the **red litmus paper** into each of the three test tubes.
* [cite_start]The solution that turns the red litmus paper **blue** is the **Basic solution** (Bases change the colour of red litmus to blue [cite: 509]).
* *Note:* The litmus paper will remain red in the other two test tubes (acid and distilled water).

**2. Prepare for the Next Step**
* You now have a piece of **blue litmus paper** (the one that changed colour in the previous step).
* You have two unidentified test tubes left: one is Acidic and one is Distilled Water.

**3. Identify the Acid**
* Dip the newly formed **blue litmus paper** into the remaining two test tubes.
* [cite_start]The solution that turns the blue litmus paper back to **red** is the **Acidic solution** (Acids change the colour of blue litmus to red [cite: 509]).

**4. Identify the Distilled Water**
* The remaining test tube is **Distilled Water**.
* *Verification:* Distilled water is neutral, so it would not have changed the colour of the original red litmus paper (in Step 1) nor the blue litmus paper (in Step 3).
