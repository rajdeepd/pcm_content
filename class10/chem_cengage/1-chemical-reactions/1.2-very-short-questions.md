10. **Butylated Hydroxy Toluene (BHT)**

    **Step-by-Step Solution:**

    1.  **Understanding Antioxidants:**
        Antioxidants are substances that prevent oxidation in molecules within cells. They combat free radicals, which can cause significant damage if present in excess.

    2.  **Identifying Synthetic Antioxidants:**
        Synthetic antioxidants are man-made compounds designed to inhibit oxidation and protect against the damaging effects of free radicals.

    3.  **Naming a Synthetic Antioxidant:**
        One well-known synthetic antioxidant is **Butylated Hydroxy Toluene (BHT)**.

    4.  **Usage of BHT:**
        BHT is commonly used in food products, particularly those containing oils and fats, to prevent spoilage. It helps maintain the quality of baked goods by preventing the oxidation of fats and oils.

    5.  **Mechanism of Action:**
        BHT works by binding to oxygen molecules, thus preventing them from reacting with fats and oils, which can lead to rancidity.

    6.  **Structure of BHT:**
        The chemical structure of BHT includes a benzene ring with hydroxyl (OH) groups and butyl groups attached, which contribute to its antioxidant properties.

    **Final Answer:**
    The name of one synthetic antioxidant is **Butylated Hydroxy Toluene (BHT)**.
