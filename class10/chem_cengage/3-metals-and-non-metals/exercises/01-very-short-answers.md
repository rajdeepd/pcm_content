# Very Short Answer Type Questions

1.  Why should the metal sulphides and carbonates be converted to metal oxides in the process of extraction of metal from them?
2.  Which one of the following is (a) the most reactive or (b) the least reactive?
    $\mathrm{Au}, \mathrm{Na}, \mathrm{Cu}, \mathrm{Ca}$
3.  What is the common feature in the electronic configuration of metal atoms?
4.  Name an ore of zinc other than zinc oxide. By what process can this ore be converted to zinc oxide?
5.  State the reason why carbon is not used to reduce the oxide of sodium and aluminium.
6.  What would happen to a copper vessel if it was left for a few days in a humid atmosphere without being cleaned?
7.  Why do shopkeepers apply oil on tools made up of iron while storing them?
8.  What are the constituents of solder alloy? Which property of solder makes it suitable for welding electrical wires?
9.  What type of reaction is involved in rusting?
10. Which chemical element is alloyed with copper to make bronze?


# Answers

1.  To remove impurities.
2.  Most reactive: Na, least reactive Au
3.  Same number of valence electrons.
4.  Zinc carbonate. Calcination.
5.  Carbon is not reactive enough to reduce the oxide of sodium and aluminium.
6.  Rusting.
7.  To prevent rusting.
8.  Tin and Lead. Malleability.
9.  Oxidation reaction.
10. Tin.
