# 1.4 Effects of Oxidation in Everyday Life

## Corrosion

- Slow eating away of metal surfaces by air, water, or chemicals.  
- Rusting of iron is a redox reaction; similar coatings form on silver (black) and copper (green).
- Effects: wastage of metals; damage to car bodies, bridges, railings, ships, etc.; costly replacements.
- Note: For aluminium, corrosion is not considered wasteful.

### Prevention

- Painting, galvanising, electroplating.

## Rancidity

- Slow oxidation of fats/oils in food, causing bad smell/taste.
- Prevention methods:
  - Keep food in airtight containers.
  - Refrigerate cooked food.
  - Pack chips/foods in nitrogen instead of air.
  - Add antioxidants to fatty foods.
