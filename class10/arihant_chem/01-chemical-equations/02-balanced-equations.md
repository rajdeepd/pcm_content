# 1.3 Balanced Chemical Equations

An equation is **skeletal** if atoms on both sides are unequal. A **balanced** equation has equal atoms of each element on both sides, consistent with the law of conservation of mass (“mass can neither be created nor destroyed during a chemical reaction”).

## Balancing by Hit-and-Trial (Example: Sodium + Water)

Unbalanced form (skeletal):  
$ \text{Na} + \text{H}_2\text{O} \longrightarrow \text{NaOH} + \text{H}_2 $

**Step I** Write the unbalanced equation and enclose formulae in brackets.  
**Step II** List atom counts: Na (1|1), H (2|3), O (1|1).  
**Step III** Balance hydrogen first: place a coefficient 2 before $\text{H}_2\text{O}$ to make H = 4 on LHS.  
**Step IV** Balance sodium next: place a coefficient 2 before NaOH to make Na = 2 on RHS.  
**Step V** Recheck other elements; all balanced.  
**Step VI** Confirm counts: Na (2|2), H (4|4), O (2|2).

Balanced equation:  
$ 2\text{Na} + 2\text{H}_2\text{O} \longrightarrow 2\text{NaOH} + \text{H}_2 $

## Worked Example: Ethane Combustion

Skeletal: $ \text{C}_2\text{H}_6 + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow \text{CO}_2 + \text{H}_2\text{O} $

| Element | LHS atoms | RHS atoms (start) |
|---|---|---|
| C | 2 | 1 |
| H | 6 | 2 |
| O | 2 | 3 |

Steps  
1) Balance H by placing 3 before $ \text{H}_2\text{O} $ → H = 6 RHS.  
2) Balance C by placing 2 before $ \text{CO}_2 $ → C = 2 RHS, O totals 2×2 + 3 = 7.  
3) Balance O by placing 7/2 before $ \text{O}_2 $ (then scale to whole numbers).  
4) Multiply all coefficients by 2 to clear the fraction.

Balanced: $ 2\text{C}_2\text{H}_6 + 7\text{O}_2 \rightarrow 4\text{CO}_2 + 6\text{H}_2\text{O} $

## Worked Example: Iron with Steam

Skeletal: $ \text{Fe} + \text{H}_2\text{O} \rightarrow \text{Fe}_3\text{O}_4 + \text{H}_2 $

| Element | LHS atoms | RHS atoms (start) |
|---|---|---|
| Fe | 1 | 3 |
| H  | 2 | 2 |
| O  | 1 | 4 |

Steps  
1) Balance O by placing 4 before $ \text{H}_2\text{O} $.  
2) Balance Fe by placing 3 before Fe.  
3) Balance H by placing 4 before $ \text{H}_2 $.  

Balanced: $ 3\text{Fe} + 4\text{H}_2\text{O} \rightarrow \text{Fe}_3\text{O}_4 + 4\text{H}_2 $

## Making Chemical Equations More Informative

Add physical states: (s) solid, (l) liquid, (g) gas, (aq) aqueous.  
Use ↓ for precipitate formation and ↑ for evolved gas when helpful.

### Conditions and Heat Notation
- Show heat/light/electricity above the arrow or as [Heat] on the right:  
  $ \text{C}(s) + \text{O}_2(g) \rightarrow \text{CO}_2(g) + \text{Heat} $  
  $ \text{CO}(g) + 2\text{H}_2(g) \xrightarrow[340\ \text{atm}]{\text{Catalyst}} \text{CH}_3\text{OH}(l) $
- Include physical states in equations:  
  $ \text{Mg}(s) + \text{H}_2\text{SO}_4(aq) \rightarrow \text{MgSO}_4(aq) + \text{H}_2(g) \uparrow $
